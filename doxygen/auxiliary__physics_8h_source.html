30 /**
31  * Au auxiliary physics is defined as a physics that is solved on top of
32  * a core physics, the latter being the Navier-Stokes equations.
33  * Auxiliary physics are simpler physics around which the entire simulation
34  * process does not need to be tailored. Examples of auxiliary physics are
35  * temperature and concentration. Generally, the auxiliary physics does not
36  * affect the core physics. For example, temperature is advected by the fluid
37  * flow, but buoyancy effects are not taken into account.
38  *
39  * The auxiliary physics are managed by the multiphysics interface of Lethe.
40  *
41  * This base class is used to establish all of the routines that an auxiliary
42  * physics must be able to provide to the Multiphysics interface. These elements
43  * are then called at specific moments of a simulation.
44  *
45  * Auxiliary physics are templated by the dimension of the problem and the
46  * vector type that is used to manage their data.
47  *
48  *
49  * Current limitations:
50  *
51  * - Auxiliary physics are currently expected to be used in conjunction with a
52  * flow solver which can provide a velocity field.
53  * - Support for feedback from the auxiliary physics to the core physics is
54  * there but presently not used anywhere
55  * - Support for interaction between auxiliary physics is supported but has not
56  * been tested
57  **/
